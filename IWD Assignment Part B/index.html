Introduction

Posted Novemeber 15th 2017


Queenstown (Māori: Tāhuna) is a renowned and favourite tourist destination in Otago in the south-west of New Zealand's South Island. It was built near an inlet called Queenstown Bay on Lake Wakatipu and surrounded by nearby mountains such as Cecil Peak, Walter Peak and Ben Lomond and Queenstown Hill. Queenstown can offer visitors a stunning scenery, huge range of activities and renowned warm and welcome atmosphere. Thus, visitors can experience a breath-taking holiday full of adventure, exploration or relaxation.


Purpose

The main purpose of this project is to develop a website which can showcase and further promote tourist destinations in Queenstown. In terms of advertising, website based promoting is a more viable alternative as it reaps both social and economic benefits for Queenstown.


Target Auidence

In terms of target audience, this website aims to capture local and overseas tourist who are looking to plan future trips to Queens Towns. It aims to provide them with up to date events, accommodation plans, and recommendations and reviews to make selecting and planning future trips easy.

Upcomming Events

Akarua Arrowtown Autumn Festival



The Akarua Arrowtown Autumn Festival is a celebration of Autumn for our community. It hosts a variety of events aimed at all ages with our most well know event being the Market Day and Parade on Saturday 22nd April 2017.

Winter Events

Queenstown Winter Festival


The Queenstown Winter Festival celebrates the start of winter like only Queenstown can, with street parties, music, comedy, family fun and madness up the mountain. On Thursday 22nd June the celebrations begin and is followed by four days of fun, jam-packed with events. From family events to DJs and nightlife - there’s something on the programme for everyone. Queenstowners have always loved winter but it was made official when the very first Queenstown Winter Festival took place in 1975.

Thrill Seeker Events

Kawarau Bridge Bungy

This is where the Bungy craze all started in 1988. The Kawarau Bridge Bungy Centre is the World Home of Bungy, situated along the picturesque Kawarau River, about a 30min drive out of Queenstown, NZ. This original Bungy site is where commercial Bungy Jumping was invented in 1988 by co-founders Henry van Asch and AJ Hackett- At the Kawarau Bridge we do a traditional foot-tie style Bungy - go at it alone or tandem Jump with a friend.
